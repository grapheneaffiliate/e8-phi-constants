# Part 1: What Is the GSM?

## 1.1 The Free Parameter Problem

The Standard Model of particle physics, despite its spectacular experimental success, contains approximately 25 free parameters: three gauge coupling constants, six quark masses, three charged lepton masses, the Higgs vacuum expectation value and self-coupling, four CKM mixing angles, a strong CP phase, and four PMNS mixing parameters. When neutrino masses and cosmological parameters are included, the count rises further. These parameters must be determined experimentally; the theory itself provides no explanation for their values.

The search for a deeper structure that *determines* these parameters has a long history. Kaluza-Klein theory demonstrated that gauge symmetry can emerge from extra-dimensional geometry. String compactification extends this idea to derive low-energy physics from the topology of compact manifolds, though the landscape of ~10^500 vacua complicates predictivity. Garrett Lisi's "An Exceptionally Simple Theory of Everything" proposed embedding the Standard Model gauge group and matter content within the 248-dimensional E8 Lie algebra, generating significant interest and debate.

## 1.2 The GSM Proposal

The central claim of the Geometric Standard Model is:

> **Physics = Geometry(E8 -> H4)**

That is, the fundamental constants of nature are geometric invariants of the unique projection from the E8 root lattice onto the H4 icosahedral Coxeter group.

**The choice of E8 -> H4 is not a free parameter of the framework -- it is forced by two mathematical theorems:**

1. **Viazovska (Fields Medal 2022)** proved that the E8 lattice is the *unique* optimal sphere packing in eight dimensions; no other lattice in any dimension d <= 8 achieves this distinction.

2. **The Elser-Sloane construction** establishes that H4 is the *unique* maximal non-crystallographic Coxeter subgroup of the E8 Weyl group, with the golden ratio phi = (1 + sqrt(5))/2 as its fundamental eigenvalue.

There is exactly one such projection. The framework has zero free parameters because the geometry has zero alternatives.

## 1.3 How the GSM Differs from Lisi

The GSM does not attempt to embed fermion representations directly into the E8 adjoint. Instead, it uses the E8 -> H4 projection to derive *numerical values* of physical constants from the Casimir degrees, Coxeter exponents, and representation-theoretic data of E8. The Coleman-Mandula objection does not apply because the GSM is not a traditional grand unified theory -- it derives coupling constants from lattice geometry rather than attempting to embed all particles in a single representation. The distinction is sharp: the GSM Lagrangian is a standard 4D gauge theory with Wilson coefficients determined by E8 geometry; fermions enter through the usual Standard Model representations, not through the E8 adjoint.

> **a1 + 11*a2 + 19*a3 + 29*a4 = 0 (mod 30), with a1+a2+a3+a4 = n**

**Theorem (Parity Constraint -- Lean 4 verified):** No w-invariant scalar polynomial of degree n exists on V_parallel for any odd n. In particular, the H4 Coxeter exponents n in {11, 19, 29} are forbidden as scalar operator degrees.

**Proof sketch:** Since all Coxeter exponents {1, 11, 19, 29} are odd, the left-hand side of the congruence has the same parity as n. For odd n, the sum is odd and can never equal 0 (mod 30). Explicit enumeration confirms zero invariant monomials at degrees 11, 19, and 29.
